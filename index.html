Robert P. Laudone

I am an NSF Postdoctoral Fellow at the University of Michigan. I'm currently on the job market for Fall 2022.

My area of research is algebraic combinatorics, representation theory and representation stability. I am interested in stability results for sequences of spaces and objects with some type of symmetry, the role of combinatorial and diagrammatic techniques in understanding these results and the ensuing implications and applications in the fields of representation theory, algebraic geometry and combinatorics.

My postdoctoral mentor is Andrew Snowden.

My Ph.D. advisors were Steven Sam and Daniel Erman.
